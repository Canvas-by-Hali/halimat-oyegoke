Getting Started with AI Art Tools

Text-to-image generation is at the heart of this movement. Tools like DALL·E, Bing Image Creator, Stable Diffusion, and MidJourney have made AI art accessible to everyone.

  • Bing Image Creator integrates DALL·E’s capabilities into its platform, providing an easy-to-use interface alongside search functionality.
  • MidJourney, popular for its distinctive aesthetic, often appeals to creators in gaming and design.
  • Stable Diffusion offers more control for those looking to refine their images and outputs.
  • Deep Dream Generator - a popular tool that can turn your photos into trippy, psychedelic works of art.
  • Artbreeder - a tool that allows you to mix and match different art styles to create something new.
  • NeuralStyler - a tool that can apply the style of one image to another, creating a unique hybrid.
Each tool has unique features and terms of use.

How exactly does licensing fit into this whole equation? To answer that question, we must first understand what a license is.
Broadly speaking, a license is a contractual agreement between two parties, where one party(the licensor) grants permission to the other party( the licensee) to use their intellectual property(IP). Now to our question, art is universally considered a form of intellectual property of an artist hence licenses are typically drawn to protect the artist's work and avoid legal repercussions. Although there are still some gray areas around the status of AI art as intellectual property, many artists and companies consider them as such. Hence, licenses are being drawn for them.
Simply put, licensing governs how your art generated by AI tools (such as the ones mentioned earlier) can be used, sold, and shared.

After the generation of our art, here are key questions we should be asking as potential sellers:

  1. Do I Have the Right to Sell This Art? Verify that the AI tool’s terms of use grant you the right to sell the generated art commercially. Some tools may have restrictions on commercial use.
  2. Is My Art Compliant with Copyright Laws? Ensure your AI-generated art does not infringe on any existing copyrights. Avoid using copyrighted materials as inputs for your AI models.
  3. Have I Disclosed the Use of AI? Platforms like Etsy require sellers to disclose if an item is created with the use of AI. Make sure your listing are transparent with AI involvement.
  4. What Type of License Should I Use? Decide on the type of license you want to apply to your art. Options include Creative Commons licenses, custom licenses, or retaining all rights. This will depend on how you want others to use your art.
  5. Am I Following Platform Guidelines? Review and comply with the specific guidelines of the platform you are using. For example, Etsy has rules about listing AI-generated art and requires transparency in your listings.
  6. Do I Need a Business License? Depending on your location, you might need a business license to sell products online. Check with your local government or a legal advisor to ensure compliance with any necessary business requirements.

By addressing these questions and understanding the licensing basics, you can confidently navigate the legal landscape of selling AI-generated art on platforms like Etsy. For example, OpenAI’s DALL·E typically allows commercial use but requires users to comply with their policies. On the other hand, some platforms may retain partial rights to the art, even if you created it. Always read the platform’s terms and conditions before selling or distributing your creations

Drafting and Using Licenses

Drafting a license for your AI-generated art can be important depending on how you intend to use and sell your work. Here are some key points to consider when drafting a license:
  1. Commercial Use: If you plan to sell your AI-generated art, especially on platforms like Etsy or Creative Fabrica, you should draft a license to clarify the terms of use for your buyers.
  2. Custom Agreements: When working with clients on custom projects or commissions, a license can outline the specific rights and limitations of use.
  3. Collaborations: If your AI-generated art involves collaboration with other artists or uses third-party resources, a license can help manage the rights and responsibilities of all parties involved.

    Types of Licenses

  1. Creative Commons (CC) Licenses: These licenses allow you to share your work with others while retaining some control over how it is used. They range from more permissive (allowing for broader use and modification) to more restrictive (limiting use to certain purposes or requiring attribution).
  2. Open Source Licenses: Licenses like the MIT license or the GNU General Public License (GPL) allow others to use, modify, and distribute the artwork under certain conditions.
  3. Public Domain: Releasing your AI-generated artwork into the public domain means it can be used, modified, and distributed without any restrictions.
  4. All Rights Reserved: Retaining all rights to your AI-generated artwork means you do not license it for use by others. This is the most restrictive option but gives you complete control over your work.
  5. Custom Licensing Agreements: Tailored to your specific needs, these agreements can combine elements from multiple licenses, offering a unique combination of permissions, restrictions, and conditions.

Platforms and Licensing Requirements

  • Etsy: Requires transparency about the use of AI in your listings but does not mandate a specific license. However, having a clear license can help manage buyer expectations and protect your rights.
  • Creative Fabrica: Allows the sale of AI-generated art but requires you to upload your creations to the designated AI-generated category. A license can clarify the terms of use for your buyers.
  • Redbubble, Society6, and Zazzle: These platforms generally handle the licensing of your art for you, but it’s still a good idea to understand their terms and ensure you have the right to sell your AI-generated art commercially.

When it comes to licensing AI-generated art, or art in general choosing the right license that caters to your needs while keeping the best interest of your customers at heart may seem overwhelming at first. It is, however, not as complicated as it initially seems to be! Remember, that you’re free to experiment with a few types before settling for one you think suits you and your vision for your art.
    Here are some questions that I believe would help in selecting a license type suitable for you:
  • Commercial Use: Will buyers be allowed to use the art for commercial purposes, like in advertisements or product designs?
  • Modifications: Are buyers allowed to alter your AI art?
  • Resale: Can buyers resell the art as part of their products, or is it for personal use only?
  • Attribution: Do you want to be credited as the creator, even if it’s AI-assisted?

Drafting Your License

You can write your license or adapt an existing template. Here’s an example that you can adapt for your AI Art Title of Work: [Insert Title of Artwork] Creator: [Your Name]
  1. License Scope This license allows the licensee (buyer) to [specify allowed uses, e.g., display, personal projects, commercial use].

  2. Restrictions

    - The artwork cannot be resold or redistributed as a standalone file.
    - Modifications to the original artwork are [permitted/not permitted].
    - The artwork must not be used for [insert restrictions, e.g., hate speech, illegal purposes].

  3. Attribution
    Credit to the creator ([Your Name]) is [required/not required] in any public use of the artwork.

  4. Ownership
    The creator retains all copyrights and moral rights to the artwork, except as explicitly granted in this license.

  5. Term of Use
    This license is [perpetual/limited to X years].

  6. Governing Law
    This license is governed by the laws of [Your Country/Region].

Choosing a Pre-Made License for AI Art

  • Creative Commons: Works well if you want to share your art freely but with specific conditions (e.g., attribution required).
  • Royalty-Free: Common for marketplaces like Shutterstock or Creative Fabrica, where buyers can use the art without ongoing fees but within clear limits.
  • Exclusive License: If a buyer wants to ensure no one else uses the same piece, offer an exclusive license with a higher price.
  • Non-Exclusive License: Allows multiple buyers to use the same artwork.

    • Where to Use These Licenses

  • On platforms like Creative Fabrica, Etsy, or your website, attach the license terms as a downloadable file or include them in the product description.
  • If working directly with clients, provide the license as part of your agreement.
    • Best Practices for AI Art Licensing
  • Be transparent about the art’s AI origin, especially if platforms or buyers ask for this information.
  • Regularly check the licensing terms of the AI tools you use, as they can change.

Legal and Ethical Considerations

The intersection of AI and copyright law remains a gray area. Here’s what you need to keep in mind:
  • Authorship Disputes: Traditional copyright laws don’t fully address AI-generated works. Is the author the person inputting the prompt or the AI platform itself?
  • Transparency: Be upfront about using AI in your work. Labeling your creations as “AI-generated” can build trust with buyers and avoid disputes.

Case Study: In 2023, an artist sued an AI tool for replicating their unique style. This highlights the importance of ethical practices, such as avoiding prompts that imitate a specific artist’s work.

Commercialization Tips for Sellers

  1. Choose the Right Platform Platforms like Etsy are excellent for selling individual pieces, while Creative Fabrica caters to recurring sales with its subscription model.
  2. Watermark Your Previews Protect your work by adding watermarks to preview images, especially when sharing on social media or platforms prone to unauthorized downloads.
  3. Bundle Your Products Group similar items (e.g., a floral clipart set) to increase value and attract buyers.
  4. Understand Your Audience Tailor your products to the platform. For instance, Etsy shoppers might prefer unique designs for home décor, while Creative Fabrica users look for versatile assets for crafting projects.
